Things people ask us

Is it legal to sell my house without an estate agent?

Completely. There is no law requiring you to use an agent — thousands of UK homes sell privately every year. You'll still use a solicitor or conveyancer for the legal work, exactly as you would with an agent. The one legal must-have is an Energy Performance Certificate (EPC) before your home is marketed.

How do buyers get in touch?

Your phone number is on your listing. Buyers call or text you directly to ask questions and book viewings. No portals, no call centres, no "your agent will get back to you".

What does it cost?

A standard listing — one photo, full description and your phone number — is free. Want up to ten photos? That's a one-off £29. A video tour and featured placement is £49. There is never a commission on the sale price.

Who handles viewings and negotiations?

You do — and that's the point. Nobody can answer questions about your home, your street and your neighbours like you can. Our free selling guide covers how to run viewings safely and handle offers with confidence.

Why only Melbourne?

Because hyperlocal works. Every buyer browsing this site is looking in Melbourne and the surrounding villages — Kings Newton, Ticknall, Wilson, Stanton by Bridge — not scrolling past your home on a national portal. We'd rather be the best in DE73 than average everywhere.
